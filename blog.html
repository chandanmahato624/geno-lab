What is Squamous Cell Carcinoma ?

Rohit Sharma on Sep 29, 2017 at 9:48 am

Squamous cell carcinoma (SCC) is a type of skin cancer that arises from the squamous cells, which are flat cells found on the surface of the skin's outer layer (epidermis). Squamous cell carcinoma can also develop in other tissues of the body, such as the lining of the digestive tract, respiratory tract, urinary tract, and other organs.

Squamous cell carcinoma typically appears as a firm, red nodule or a flat lesion with a scaly or crusty surface. It may also resemble a wart or an open sore that does not heal. Early detection and treatment are crucial for preventing the spread of squamous cell carcinoma to other parts of the body.

People with fair skin are more susceptible to UV damage and have a higher risk of developing skin cancer.

John Dow

Treatment options for squamous cell carcinoma depend on various factors, including the size and location of the tumor, as well as the individual's overall health. Common treatments include surgical excision, Mohs surgery, radiation therapy, and topical medications. In some cases, targeted therapy or immunotherapy may be recommended, especially for advanced or recurrent SCC.
